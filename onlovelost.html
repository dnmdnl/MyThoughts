On Love Lost.

"I was happier with you, even if we never met"

It was a cold day when the month started out.
A broken thought with every silenced opening of my mouth.
Afraid of the uncertainty of what might happen and what's to come.
Then here comes a kind heart on a screen -- asking 'Is she the one?'

For months I have thought that you were just an example of who I want.
A figment of my imagination -- an impossible hunt.
Then one day I figured out that you are not just an example but a need for me.
A distant soul but will always seek out to see.

I became as calm and quiet unlike the nights before my chained release.
I guess that's the reason why I called you my peace.
You became the reason why I wanted to exist.
So don't ever say you're unworthy -- you're perfect to say the least.

So I wanted to say thank you for being there.
And I'm sorry for being unfair.
I should've told you sooner -- or never.
Because losing you would scar me forever.

This poem was just supposed to tell you that I'm happy.
And believe me -- You're someone I'll never regret.
I hope that in your heart that you'd keep me.
Because to be painfully honest ...
I was happier with you -- even if we never met.
